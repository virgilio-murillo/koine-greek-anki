## 1. GREEK SUFFIXES: Comprehensive Reference

### 1.1 -τός (verbal adjective: "capable of being X-ed" or "X-ed")

**Function:** Creates adjectives from verb roots, expressing possibility or completed state. Equivalent to English "-able/-ible" or past participles. (Smyth §858; Mounce, *Morphology of Biblical Greek*)

**Formation rule:** Verb root (often zero-grade/weak form) + -τός

**NT Examples from project data:**
- **ἐκλεκτός** = ἐκ (out) + λεκ- (root of λέγω in sense "choose") + -τός → "chosen out" = elected
- **συνετός** = σύν (with) + ε- (root of ἵημι "send/put together") + -τός → "put together" = intelligent
- **ἀνεκτός** = ἀνά (up) + εκ- (root of ἔχω "hold") + -τός → "bearable" = tolerable

**Key insight for cards:** The -τός suffix signals "this is an adjective derived from a verb." The root often appears in a reduced form compared to the dictionary verb.

### 1.2 -σις (action/process noun: "the act of X-ing")

**Function:** Creates abstract nouns denoting the action or process of the verb. Equivalent to English "-tion/-sis." (Smyth §840; Peter Smith, *Greek and Latin Roots* §135)

**Formation rule:** Verb root (often e-grade) + -σις

**NT Examples:**
- **ἀνάστασις** = ἀνά (up) + στα- (root of ἵστημι "stand") + -σις → "standing up" = resurrection
- **πρόθεσις** = πρό (before) + θε- (root of τίθημι "place") + -σις → "placing before" = purpose/showbread
- **κατασκήνωσις** = κατά (down) + σκηνή (tent) + -ωσις → "settling down in a tent" = nest/dwelling
- **ἀνάπαυσις** = ἀνά (back) + παυ- (root of παύω "cease") + -σις → "causing to cease" = rest
- **ἀπάντησις** = ἀπό (from) + ἀντ- (root of ἀντάω "meet") + -σις → "meeting from" = encounter
- **συντέλεια** = σύν (together) + τελ- (root of τελέω "complete") + -εια → "completing together" = consummation

**Note:** -εια is a variant of -ία that functions similarly to -σις for some verbs.

### 1.3 -μα (result noun: "the thing X-ed")

**Function:** Creates concrete nouns denoting the result or product of an action. Contrasts with -σις (process) vs -μα (product). (Smyth §841; Smith §135)

**Formation rule:** Verb root + -μα (neuter noun, gen. -ματος)

**NT Examples:**
- **καταπέτασμα** = κατά (down) + πετα- (root of πετάννυμι "spread") + -μα → "thing spread down" = veil/curtain
- **ἀντάλλαγμα** = ἀντί (in exchange) + ἀλλαγ- (root of ἀλλάσσω "change") + -μα → "thing exchanged" = ransom price

**Other common NT -μα words:** βάπτισμα (thing dipped = baptism), κρίμα (thing judged = judgment), πνεῦμα (thing breathed = spirit), ῥῆμα (thing spoken = word), σῶμα (body), αἷμα (blood).

**Key contrast for cards:**
- -σις = the ACTION: πρόθεσις = "the act of placing before"
- -μα = the RESULT: πρόθεμα would be "the thing placed before"

### 1.4 -ία / -εία (quality/state noun: "the state of being X")

**Function:** Creates abstract nouns denoting a quality, state, or condition. Very productive in Koine. (Smyth §840.3)

**Formation rule:** Adjective/noun stem + -ία

**NT Examples:**
- **ἀκαθαρσία** = ἀ- (without) + καθαρός (clean) + -ία → "state of being unclean" = impurity
- **ἀνομία** = ἀ- (without) + νόμος (law) + -ία → "state of being lawless" = iniquity
- **ἀπιστία** = ἀ- (without) + πιστός (faithful) + -ία → "state of being unfaithful" = unbelief
- **εὐδοκία** = εὐ (well) + δοκέω (think) + -ία → "state of thinking well" = good pleasure
- **εὐκαιρία** = εὐ (good) + καιρός (moment) + -ία → "state of good timing" = opportunity
- **πολυλογία** = πολύς (much) + λόγος (word) + -ία → "state of many words" = wordiness

**Pattern:** ἀ-/ἀν- + noun/adj + -ία is extremely productive for "lack of X" nouns.

### 1.5 -μός (action noun: "the process of X-ing")

**Function:** Creates masculine nouns denoting an action or process, often with more concrete/vivid connotation than -σις. (Smyth §840.2)

**Formation rule:** Verb root + -μός (or -σμός/-ισμός)

**NT Examples:**
- **κατακλυσμός** = κατά (over/down) + κλυσ- (root of κλύζω "flood") + -μός → "flooding over" = deluge
- **διαλογισμός** = διά (through) + λογισ- (root of λογίζομαι "reason") + -μός → "reasoning through" = thought/deliberation
- **βαπτισμός** = βαπτ- (root of βάπτω "dip") + -ισμός → "the dipping" = baptism/washing

### 1.6 -ή (action/result noun)

**Function:** Creates feminine nouns from verb roots, denoting either the action or its result. One of the most common noun-forming suffixes. (Smyth §840.5)

**Formation rule:** Verb root (often o-grade) + -ή

**NT Examples:**
- **καταβολή** = κατά (down) + βολ- (o-grade of βάλλω "throw") + -ή → "throwing down" = foundation
- **παραβολή** = παρά (beside) + βολ- (o-grade of βάλλω) + -ή → "throwing beside" = parable/comparison
- **ἀνατολή** = ἀνά (up) + τολ- (root of τέλλω "rise") + -ή → "rising up" = east/sunrise
- **ἐντολή** = ἐν (in) + τολ- (root of τέλλω "accomplish") + -ή → "accomplishment within" = commandment
- **συναγωγή** = σύν (together) + ἀγωγ- (o-grade of ἄγω "lead") + -ή → "leading together" = synagogue
- **προσευχή** = πρός (toward) + εὐχ- (root of εὔχομαι "pray") + -ή → "praying toward" = prayer
- **ἐπιστροφή** = ἐπί (upon) + στροφ- (o-grade of στρέφω "turn") + -ή → "turning upon" = conversion
- **ἐπιταγή** = ἐπί (upon) + ταγ- (root of τάσσω "order") + -ή → "ordering upon" = command
- **ὑποστροφή** = ὑπό (back) + στροφ- (o-grade of στρέφω) + -ή → "turning back" = return

### 1.7 -ικός (relational adjective: "having the character of X")

**Function:** Creates adjectives from nouns or verb roots, meaning "pertaining to" or "characterized by." Source of English "-ic." (Smith §105)

**Formation rule:** Noun/verb stem + -ικός

**NT Example:**
- **παραλυτικός** = παρά (beside) + λυ- (root of λύω "loosen") + -τικός → "characterized by loosening beside" = paralytic

**Note:** -τικός (from verb roots) vs -ικός (from noun stems) — both yield English "-ic" but have different Greek bases.

### 1.8 -ιος (belonging/relational adjective: "belonging to X")

**Function:** Creates adjectives meaning "belonging to" or "connected with." (Smyth §858.5)

**NT Examples:**
- **παραθαλάσσιος** = παρά (beside) + θάλασσα (sea) + -ιος → "belonging to the seaside" = by the sea
- **ἐπιούσιος** = ἐπί (upon) + οὐσία (substance/being) + -ιος → "belonging to what is needed" = daily
- **ἀκέραιος** = ἀ- (without) + κεράννυμι (mix) + -ιος → "unmixed" = pure/innocent
- **ἀναίτιος** = ἀν- (without) + αἴτιος (guilty) → "without guilt" = innocent

### 1.9 -ών (place/agent noun)

**Function:** Creates masculine nouns, often denoting a place or (less commonly) an agent. (Smyth §843)

**NT Examples:**
- **ἀφεδρών** = ἀπό→ἀφ- (away) + ἕδρα (seat) + -ών → "away-seat" = latrine
- **ἀμπελών** = ἄμπελος (vine) + -ών → "place of vines" = vineyard
- **αἰών** = (related to αἰεί "always") + -ών → "age/eternity"

### 1.10 -τήρ / -τής (agent noun: "one who X-es")

**Function:** Creates masculine nouns denoting the person who performs an action. -τήρ is older/poetic; -τής is the standard Koine form. (Smyth §839)

**NT Examples:**
- **ὑποκριτής** = ὑπό (under) + κρι- (root of κρίνω "judge/act") + -τής → "one who acts under" = actor → hypocrite
- **ψευδοπροφήτης** = ψευδής (false) + προφήτης (one who speaks forth) → false prophet
- **βαπτιστής** = βαπτ- (root of βάπτω "dip") + -ιστής → "one who dips" = baptizer

**-τήρ vs -τής distinction:** In Classical Greek, -τήρ often implied a habitual/professional agent while -τής was more general. By Koine, -τής had largely replaced -τήρ. The NT uses σωτήρ (savior) as one of the few surviving -τήρ forms.

## 2. PREFIX SOUND CHANGES (Allomorphy)

When Greek prefixes combine with roots, they undergo predictable phonological changes. These are critical for recognizing compound words.

### 2.1 ἀπό (away from)
| Environment | Form | Example | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before consonant | ἀπο- | ἀπο-στέλλω → ἀποστέλλω | No change |
| Before smooth vowel | ἀπ- | ἀπο- + αἴρω → ἀπαίρω | Final -ο drops (elision) |
| Before rough breathing | ἀφ- | ἀπο- + ἵημι → ἀφίημι | -ο drops + π aspirates to φ |

**Project data:** 37 ἀπό- compounds found. The component always shows "ἀπό" but actual word forms include ἀφ- (ἀφεδρών, ἀφίημι) and ἀπ- forms.

### 2.2 ἐκ (out of)
| Environment | Form | Example | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before consonant | ἐκ- | ἐκ-βάλλω → ἐκβάλλω | No change |
| Before vowel | ἐξ- | ἐκ + ὁμολογέω → ἐξομολογέω | κ → ξ before vowel |

**Project data:** 16 ἐκ- compounds. Note: ἐκκλησία shows ἐκ + καλέω with assimilation (κκ).

### 2.3 σύν (with, together)
| Environment | Form | Example | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before β, μ, π, φ | συμ- | σύν + βάλλω → συμβάλλω | ν → μ (labial assimilation) |
| Before γ, κ, χ, ξ | συγ- | σύν + γνώμη → συγγνώμη | ν → γ (velar assimilation) |
| Before λ | συλ- | σύν + λαμβάνω → συλλαμβάνω | ν → λ (liquid assimilation) |
| Before ρ | συρ- | σύν + ῥέω → συρρέω | ν → ρ |
| Before σ, ζ | συσ-/συ- | σύν + στρέφω → συστρέφω | ν drops or assimilates |
| Before vowel/other | συν- | σύν + ἄγω → συνάγω | No change |

**Project data:** 15 σύν- compounds. All show "σύν" in components but actual forms include: συναγωγή, συνετός, συντέλεια, etc.

### 2.4 μετά (after, with, change)
| Environment | Form | Example | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before consonant | μετα- | μετα-νοέω → μετανοέω | No change |
| Before smooth vowel | μετ- | μετα- + οἰκίζω → μετοικίζω | Elision |
| Before rough breathing | μεθ- | μετα- + ἵστημι → μεθίστημι | Elision + aspiration |

**Project data:** 5 μετά- compounds (μετανοέω, μεταβαίνω, μεταίρω, μεταμέλομαι, μετοικεσία).

### 2.5 κατά (down, against, completely)
| Environment | Form | Example | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before consonant | κατα- | κατα-βαίνω → καταβαίνω | No change |
| Before smooth vowel | κατ- | κατα- + ἀξιόω → καταξιόω | Elision |
| Before rough breathing | καθ- | κατα- + ἵστημι → καθίστημι | Elision + aspiration |

**Project data:** 27 κατά- compounds. Largest group after ἀπό-.

### 2.6 ἐπί (upon, on)
| Environment | Form | Example | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before consonant | ἐπι- | ἐπι-γινώσκω → ἐπιγινώσκω | No change |
| Before smooth vowel | ἐπ- | ἐπι- + αἰσχύνω → ἐπαισχύνομαι | Elision |
| Before rough breathing | ἐφ- | ἐπι- + ἵστημι → ἐφίστημι | Elision + aspiration |

**Project data:** 24 ἐπί- compounds.

### 2.7 ἐν (in)
| Environment | Form | Example | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before β, μ, π, φ | ἐμ- | ἐν + βάλλω → ἐμβάλλω | Nasal assimilation |
| Before γ, κ, χ | ἐγ- | ἐν + καλέω → ἐγκαλέω | Nasal assimilation |
| Before λ | ἐλ- | ἐν + λείπω → ἐλλείπω | Liquid assimilation |
| Before ρ | ἐρ- | ἐν + ῥίπτω → ἐρρίπτω | Liquid assimilation |
| Before vowel/dental | ἐν- | ἐν + τέλλω → ἐντέλλω | No change |

**Project data:** 9 ἐν- compounds.

### General Rules (Source: Smyth §77-80)
1. **Elision:** Prefixes ending in a vowel (ἀπό, μετά, κατά, ἐπί, παρά, ἀνά) drop the vowel before a root starting with a vowel.
2. **Aspiration:** When elision brings a voiceless stop (π, τ, κ) next to a rough breathing (ʽ), the stop aspirates: π→φ, τ→θ, κ→χ.
3. **Nasal assimilation:** ν (in σύν, ἐν) assimilates to the place of articulation of the following consonant.

## 3. VERB ROOT CHANGES IN DERIVED NOUNS/ADJECTIVES

Greek verbs exhibit "ablaut" (vowel gradation) inherited from Proto-Indo-European. The dictionary form (present tense) often has a different root shape than the form appearing in derived nouns. Understanding these patterns is essential for connecting compound nouns back to their source verbs.

### 3.1 Ablaut Grades Explained

| Grade | Vowel | Example with βαλ-/βολ-/βλη- |
|---|---|---|
| **e-grade** (normal) | ε | βάλλω (present: "I throw") |
| **o-grade** (strengthened) | ο | βολ-ή (noun: "a throw") |
| **zero-grade** (reduced) | ∅ | βλη- (aorist passive: ἐβλήθην) |
| **lengthened e-grade** | η | βλῆ-μα (rare) |

(Source: OSU Greek Word Formation page; Smith §135; Smyth §476)

### 3.2 Specific Verb Root Mappings for NT Compounds

#### βάλλω "throw" → root βαλ-/βολ-
- Present: βάλλω (with nasal infix -λλ-)
- **o-grade in nouns:** βολ-ή
- **καταβολή** = κατά + βολ- + -ή → "foundation" (throwing down)
- **παραβολή** = παρά + βολ- + -ή → "parable" (throwing beside)
- **Card explanation:** "βάλλω means 'throw.' In nouns, the root changes from βαλ- to βολ- (like English sing→song). So παρα-βολ-ή = 'a throwing-beside' = comparison = parable."

#### τίθημι "place" → root θε-/θη-
- Present: τίθημι (with reduplication τι-)
- **e-grade in nouns:** θε-σις
- **πρόθεσις** = πρό + θε- + -σις → "purpose" (placing before)
- **ἀποθήκη** = ἀπό + θη- + -κη → "storehouse" (place for putting away)
- **διαθήκη** = διά + θη- + -κη → "covenant" (arrangement placed through)
- **Card explanation:** "τίθημι means 'place/put.' The root is θε- (seen in πρό-θε-σις 'placing before') or θη- (seen in ἀπο-θή-κη 'place for storing'). The τι- at the start of τίθημι is just reduplication, not part of the root."

#### ἵστημι "stand/set up" → root στα-/στη-
- Present: ἵστημι (with reduplication ἱ-)
- **a-grade in nouns:** στα-σις
- **ἀνάστασις** = ἀνά + στα- + -σις → "resurrection" (standing up)
- **ἀποστάσιον** = ἀπό + στα- + -σιον → "certificate of divorce" (standing away)
- **Card explanation:** "ἵστημι means 'stand/set up.' The root is στα- (the ἱ- is reduplication). So ἀνά-στα-σις = 'standing up again' = resurrection."

#### στέλλω "send/equip" → root στελ-/στολ-
- Present: στέλλω (with nasal infix -λλ-)
- **o-grade in nouns:** στολ-
- **ἀπόστολος** = ἀπό + στολ- + -ος → "apostle" (one sent away)
- **Card explanation:** "στέλλω means 'send.' In nouns, the root changes from στελ- to στολ- (o-grade). So ἀπό-στολ-ος = 'one sent away' = apostle."

#### ἵημι "send/let go" → root ε-/η-
- Present: ἵημι (with reduplication ἱ-)
- **e-grade in compounds:** -ε- (very short root!)
- **συνίημι** = σύν + ἵημι → "put together" = understand
- **συνετός** = σύν + ε- + -τός → "put together" = intelligent
- **ἀφίημι** = ἀπό→ἀφ- + ἵημι → "send away" = forgive
- **Card explanation:** "ἵημι means 'send/release.' Its root is just ε-/η- (the ἱ- is reduplication). In συν-ε-τός, the tiny root ε- is sandwiched between prefix and suffix: 'put-together-able' = intelligent."

#### λέγω "say/choose" → roots λεγ-/λεκ-/λογ-
- **λέγω** has two distinct meanings: "say" and "choose/gather"
- **e-grade:** λεγ- (present)
- **o-grade:** λογ- (in λόγος "word/reason")
- **zero-grade:** λεκ- (in ἐκ-λεκ-τός "chosen")
- **ἐκλεκτός** = ἐκ + λεκ- + -τός → "chosen out" = elect
- **Note on ἐκκλησία:** From ἐκ + καλέω "call" (not λέγω). Root καλ-/κλη-. ἐκ-κλη-σία = "called out" = assembly.
- **Card explanation:** "λέγω can mean 'choose' (not just 'say'). The root λεκ- appears in ἐκ-λεκ-τός = 'chosen-out' = elect. The o-grade λογ- gives us λόγος = 'word.'"

#### ἄγω "lead" → root ἀγ-/ἀγωγ-
- **o-grade in nouns:** ἀγωγ-
- **συναγωγή** = σύν + ἀγωγ- + -ή → "leading together" = synagogue
- **Card explanation:** "ἄγω means 'lead.' In nouns, the root extends to ἀγωγ-. So συν-αγωγ-ή = 'a leading-together' = gathering place = synagogue."

## 4. GAP ANALYSIS: Current Project vs. Needed Morphology

### What the project already does well:
- All 290 compounds have prefix + root decomposition
- Meanings in Spanish with mnemonic chains (e.g., "lanzar-hacia-abajo → echar cimientos")
- First NT reference for each word
- Part of speech tagging

### What's MISSING for self-contained cards:

1. **Suffix identification:** Cards don't explicitly name or explain the suffix. A card for καταβολή shows κατά + βάλλω but doesn't explain that -ή is a noun-forming suffix or that βολ- is the o-grade of βαλ-.

2. **Root form explanation:** When the root changes (βάλλω → βολ-), the card shows the dictionary verb but doesn't explain WHY the form is different. A beginner seeing "βάλλω" and "βολή" won't understand the connection.

3. **Prefix allomorph notes:** Cards show the base prefix (ἀπό) but don't note when the actual word uses an allomorph (ἀφ- in ἀφεδρών). The card for ἀφεδρών correctly shows ἀπό but a learner seeing ἀφ- in the actual word needs to know the rule.

4. **Suffix meaning field:** No field explains what the suffix contributes to meaning (e.g., -σις = "the act of," -μα = "the result of").

### Recommended additions to card template:
```
suffix: "-ή"
suffix_meaning: "sustantivo de acción/resultado"
root_note: "βολ- es la forma nominal de βάλλω (grado-o: α→ο)"
prefix_note: null  // or "ἀπό → ἀφ- ante aspiración"
```

## 5. FLASHCARD PEDAGOGY: Best Practices for Morphology

### 5.1 Research-backed principles

**From spaced repetition research and Greek pedagogy sources:**

1. **Minimum effective information** (Wozniak's 20 rules): Each card should test ONE piece of knowledge. Don't put all morphology on one card. (Source: supermemo.com/en/articles/20rules)

2. **Morpheme awareness aids retention:** Research on L2 vocabulary acquisition shows that learners who understand word parts retain vocabulary 40-60% better than rote memorizers. (Source: Nation, *Learning Vocabulary in Another Language*, Cambridge UP)

3. **Progressive disclosure:** Start with the simple decomposition (prefix + root = meaning), then introduce suffix patterns and root changes as the learner encounters more examples. (Source: ntgreeketal.com Anki methodology)

4. **Pattern recognition over rules:** Rather than teaching "the o-grade of βαλ- is βολ-," show multiple examples (καταβολή, παραβολή, ἐπιβολή) and let the pattern emerge. (Source: Mounce, *Morphology of Biblical Greek*, Zondervan)

### 5.2 Recommended detail levels

**Level 1 (Beginner - current cards):**
- Front: Greek compound word
- Back: prefix meaning + root verb meaning → compound meaning
- Example: καταβολή = κατά (down) + βάλλω (throw) → foundation

**Level 2 (Intermediate - recommended addition):**
- Add suffix label: "Suffix -ή = action/result noun"
- Add root note: "Root βολ- is the noun form of βάλλω"
- Add one parallel: "Compare: παραβολή (throw-beside = parable)"

**Level 3 (Advanced - optional):**
- Ablaut grade explanation
- Full derivation chain
- Cross-references to cognates

### 5.3 What to AVOID on beginner cards:
- PIE reconstructions (*gʷelH-)
- Technical ablaut terminology (e-grade, o-grade, zero-grade)
- Multiple competing etymologies
- Exhaustive lists of all allomorphs

### 5.4 What HELPS on beginner cards:
- Simple "the root changes from X to Y in nouns" notes
- Parallel examples showing the same pattern (βολή family)
- Brief suffix meaning ("suffix -ή makes action nouns")
- Mnemonic connections to English cognates where available (e.g., "apostle" from ἀπόστολος)
